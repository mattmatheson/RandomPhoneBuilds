1. His Stylist — Mark Avery

Avery's signature move: suede bomber, white tee, slim jeans, boots. Timeless.
City-chic: navy chinos, striped shirt, brown brogues, tortoiseshell sunglasses

Mark Avery has been Gosling's stylist since around 2015. He started as a set costumer and evolved into Gosling's personal stylist and creative consultant. Avery recently launched his own workwear-inspired collection called Avery Ranch, rooted in classic silhouettes: canvas jackets, structured shirts, durable pants, and trucker hats.

Avery's litmus test: "If you photograph this outfit in black and white, does it look like it could be James Dean?" If it can't pass that test, it doesn't make the cut.

His Philosophy

  • Timelessness over trends. No logos, no branding, nothing fussy.
  • Vintage pieces and small-business craftsmanship over big luxury houses (though Gucci gets the formal nod).
  • "Palm reading" — Avery reads a client's energy and how they see themselves, then builds around that. He says "Ryan styles Ryan, and I just help."
  • The Canadian Tuxedo is encouraged. Denim on denim is a feature, not a bug.

The core message: Gosling's look is deliberately un-trendy. Denim jackets, straight-fit jeans, boots, aviators, a pendant necklace. Stuff any non-celebrity can pull off. The magic is in the fit and the restraint.

Travel style: charcoal jeans, pale grey tee, brown shoes. The Avery uniform in action.

3. Hair — Cuts, Products & What to Tell Your Barber

Gosling has naturally fine, straight hair — which is actually ideal for his signature looks because fine hair holds shape well without getting heavy or poofy. If you have similar hair, you're already halfway there.

Every major Ryan Gosling haircut from 2004 to 2023 — notice how the silhouette stays consistent even as the styling changes

Signature Looks by Era

Drive (2011)

Textured Ivy League variation. Natural and effortless. About 2 inches on top, scissor-cut sides. Matte finish. This is the "I just woke up looking this good" cut.

Crazy, Stupid, Love (2011)

Slicked side part with length at the fringe. The "transformation" haircut from the movie. Gentleman's cut meets Ivy League — side part, tapered sides, pomade for hold.

La La Land (2016)

70s-inspired slick back, 4–5 inches on top. Water-based pomade for the shine. Very retro-romantic.

Blade Runner 2049 (2017)

Heavily textured crop, about 2 inches on top. Matte finish. Minimal effort, maximum cool. Probably his most wearable everyday look.

Barbie / Ken (2023)

Platinum blonde curtains/fringe. The full Ken transformation. Obviously don't do this unless you're being paid millions, but the texture and length are instructive.

Barbie Press Tour (2023)

Highlighted, softly textured, side-swept. Warmer blonde tones. A more relaxed, lived-in version of the CSL style. This is the one to copy if you want highlights.

The Notebook (2004)

Long, untamed shaggy locks. The full romantic lead. Requires 6+ months of growth and a willingness to let it do its thing.

First Man (2018)

Military-adjacent buzz/crew hybrid. The shortest he's gone. No-maintenance, no-product territory. Good if you want to reset and grow out fresh.

What to Tell Your Barber

Don't just say "give me the Ryan Gosling." Here's what to actually ask for:

  • For the Drive / Blade Runner look: "Scissor-cut sides blended to about a 3 or 4 guard. Leave 1.5 to 2 inches on top. Point-cut the top for texture — chop into it so there are different lengths throughout. Work with my natural part, not against it."
  • For the Crazy Stupid Love look: "Gentleman's cut meets Ivy League. Side part, tapered sides, enough length on top to slick to the side with some volume."
  • For the La La Land look: "Leave 4 to 5 inches on top, taper the sides. I want to be able to slick it back but have it fall forward naturally."
  • General: Always ask for point cutting on top. This is what gives Gosling's hair that natural, textured movement instead of a blunt, helmet-y finish.

Products for Fine Hair

The golden rule: less is more. Fine hair gets weighed down fast. Use small amounts and build up.

The fine-hair Gosling routine: Towel-dry hair until slightly damp. Hit roots with sea salt spray. Blow-dry on medium heat while running fingers through (not a brush — you want texture, not smoothness). Rub a dime-sized amount of matte clay between palms until warm, then work through the top and sides. Done. Under 5 minutes.

What Makes His Suits Work

  • Shoulders sit exactly at the edge of his natural shoulder — just enough padding for structure without bulk.
  • Subtle nip at the waist for a V-shape silhouette. Neither tight nor oversized — an athletic fit with clean drape.
  • Signature move: No tie, top buttons of the shirt open. Always.
  • Non-standard colors: Maroon, dark hunter green, grey Prince of Wales check, mint green, pastel pink. He rarely does basic navy or charcoal.
  • Details: Piping on lapels, tonal embroidery, jewel-tone velvets for awards season.

Casual Wear — The Gosling Uniform

This is where you'll actually dress like him day-to-day. His casual wardrobe is shockingly simple.

The Gosling casual uniform: black jacket, grey henley, dark jeans, boots
Layered: wine V-neck, wool jacket, chinos, pendant necklace
2023 street style — denim jacket, white tee baseline

T-Shirts

Plain white fitted tees, black tees, striped tees, henley shirts. No graphics, no logos. A well-fitting plain white tee is doing more work than any designer piece.

Jeans

Straight-fit dark denim. Never skinny, never baggy. Classic indigo or raw denim wash. This is critical — the fit between the knee and the ankle is what separates "looks put together" from "doesn't care." Straight, not tapered, not flared.

Shirts

Checked flannel (untucked), chambray, simple button-downs with rolled sleeves. Nothing fancy.

Denim Jackets

A Gosling staple. Classic blue, often layered denim-on-denim (the Canadian Tuxedo). If you're from Canada like him, apparently this is a birthright.

Brands He Actually Wears Casually

Levi's — his stylist Mark Avery did a 501 Day collaboration with them. Basic, well-made staples rather than designer labels for casual wear. The message: spend money on fit and quality fabric, not logos.

5. Key Iconic Looks

1

Drive (2011) — The Scorpion Jacket

White satin scorpion-embroidered bomber, henley underneath, dark jeans, boots. Launched a thousand Halloween costumes and an entire replica jacket industry. The jacket that proved a single bold piece over simple basics is all you need.

2

Crazy, Stupid, Love (2011) — The Makeover

Fitted V-neck sweaters, slim suits, leather shoes. This movie's wardrobe became the literal template for every "dress better" article of the 2010s. The scene where he takes Steve Carell shopping is essentially a style tutorial.

3

The Place Beyond the Pines (2012)

Bleached blonde hair, blood-red leather jacket, chain, motorcycle boots. Raw and dangerous. The polar opposite of his usual refinement — proof he can pull off anything.

4

La La Land (2016) — Retro Romance

Persol PO0714 folding sunglasses, casual button-downs, slim trousers, loafers. Retro-romantic LA cool. The color palette — warm earth tones, cream, rust — is peak Gosling territory.

5

Blade Runner 2049 (2017) — Officer K

The shearling-collar long coat over minimalist base layers. Dystopian meets timeless. This coat alone spawned a cottage industry of replicas and inspired a wave of shearling-collar outerwear.

6

Barbie (2023) — Peak Ken Energy

Pink plaid, sailor stripes, ski suits, space suits. The entire Barbie wardrobe was maximalist camp played with a straight face. Self-aware and fun without abandoning craft.

7

2024 Oscars — Red Carpet

Custom black Gucci suit with beaded embroidered trim. Hot pink socks as a subtle Barbie nod. The detail-in-the-details approach — reads classic from a distance, rewards a closer look.

8

2024 Oscars — "I'm Just Ken"

Crystal-encrusted hot pink Gucci suit, matching gloves, Barbie sunglasses. A Marilyn Monroe "Diamonds Are a Girl's Best Friend" homage. One of the most memorable Oscars fashion moments in recent history.

9

Everyday Street Style — The Uniform

White tee or henley, dark straight-fit jeans, denim jacket or leather jacket, Red Wing boots, aviators, pendant necklace. This is what he wears when nobody's paying him. This is the look to copy.

7. Style Principles — Why It Works

These are the underlying rules that make Gosling's style look effortless. Memorize these and you can build outfits without copying specific looks.

01

Fit Above Everything

This is rule number one. Nothing else matters if it doesn't fit. Gosling's shoulders lie flat, jackets never hang forward or flare out, and trousers break cleanly at the shoe. He has the confidence to wear a pink velvet suit because the tailoring is impeccable. Priority order: fit > fabric > brand > trend. A $50 shirt that fits perfectly beats a $500 shirt that doesn't.

02

Low-Contrast, Warm Color Palette

Gosling has warm undertones and low-contrast features (sandy hair, light eyes). His best colors are warm and muted: browns, tans, olive greens, warm greys, navy, burgundy, and cream. When he wears cool tones, they work against his features. When he leans warm, everything lights up. If you have similar coloring, follow this same palette. Avoid stark black-and-white combos — go for tonal, unified palettes instead.

03

Simplicity with One Focal Point

Most Gosling outfits have one interesting element and the rest is restrained. A bold suit color with no tie and minimal accessories. A plain white tee with a great leather jacket. He never stacks multiple statements. If the jacket is loud, the shirt is quiet. If the suit is colorful, the accessories are invisible. Pick one thing to be the star of the outfit.

04

Slightly Elongated Silhouette

At 6 feet with a moderate build, Gosling dresses to maintain a sleek vertical line. Jackets hit right at the hip — never longer. No harsh horizontal breaks. Monochromatic or tonal outfits extend the line and make you look taller and leaner. If you're around the same build, this is the single easiest trick to steal.

05

The "Dramatic Classic" Framework

Style analysts classify Gosling as a "dramatic classic" body type — a harmonious middle ground that benefits from garments that are restrained, well-fitted, slightly elongated, and crisp. Neither boxy nor ultra-slim. The ideal is balance, unity, and sleekness with a touch of angular sophistication.

06

Timelessness Over Trends

The Mark Avery test: could this photo be from 1965, 1985, or 2025? If yes, it works. Gosling avoids anything that screams a specific year. No loud logos, no fast-fashion trends, no streetwear hype pieces. His wardrobe from 10 years ago still looks current because he never chased what was "in."

07

Confidence to Play

Despite the "timeless" baseline, Gosling is not afraid of pink suits, triple-stacked watches, or scorpion jackets when the context calls for it. The foundation of basics gives him room to take risks without looking costumey. Once you have the fundamentals down, you've earned the right to break the rules when it's fun.
