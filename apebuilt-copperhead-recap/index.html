1Downloading the file

Hit the black Download from Google Drive button up top. That opens the file in Drive.

  • On desktop: click the download arrow in the top right of Drive's preview window. The 4K master is about 4.5 GB — give it a few minutes on Wi-Fi.
  • On phone: tap the three dots (···) and pick Send a copy → Save Video to drop it in your camera roll. Heads up: a 4 GB file takes a while over cell — do this on Wi-Fi.
  • If Drive shows a "can't scan for viruses, file too large" warning, that's normal — just hit Download anyway.

2Posting to Instagram

The video is 16:9 (cinematic). Instagram will letterbox it on a normal feed post, or you can crop it to 9:16 / 4:5 in the IG editor.

  • Reels (recommended): upload as a Reel — IG handles the crop and gets you the most reach.
  • Feed post: upload from the camera roll, pick the 16:9 sizing option, write a caption, tag @mattmatheson_ in the credits and you're good to go.
  • Story: same drill, but pick a tight section — Stories are 15s per slide.
