A WORD FROM PATRICK HALLER

We don't maintain landscapes.
We build them. At scale.

0 Years installing commercial ground in Tennessee · 1997 → 2026

For 29 years, Patrick Haller and the team have specialized in one thing: large-scale commercial landscape installation across the Upper Cumberland.

In 2019, we made a deliberate choice — to stop maintenance work entirely, and double down on what we do best. Building ground that holds. Hardscape that drains. Irrigation that runs without you thinking about it.

You won't find us doing residential mowing routes. You'll find us on TDOT projects, university campuses, municipal parks, and corporate sites where the work has to last decades.

FIG. 04 PERVIOUS · IN PROGRESS
Real differentiation · Not greenwashing

Pervious paving that actually drains.

Most contractors will sell you a stamped-concrete plaza and call it sustainable. We engineer permeable hardscape systems — high-PSI stone, polymeric joint stabilization, ADA-compliant walking surfaces — that absorb stormwater on-site instead of dumping it into the watershed.

It's the kind of thing nobody asks for in the RFP. It's the kind of thing the EPA, your insurance carrier, and the building you're working on will all thank you for in fifteen years.

How a project moves from RFP to ribbon-cutting.

Four steps · One point of contact
STEP 01

Consultation

Site walk, soil sampling, drainage assessment, and an honest read on scope. We tell you what we can do, what we can't, and what the budget actually buys.

STEP 02

Design

Custom landscape plan, hardscape layout, irrigation schematic. Native species selection. Phasing strategy if the site is live.

STEP 03

Build

In-house crews, in-house arborists, locally-sourced materials wherever possible. Single point of contact through the entire install.

STEP 04

Verify

Walk-through, irrigation wet-check, plant warranty period. If something fails in the establishment window, we replace it.
