Posting Best Practices

How to get the most out of professionally produced content on Instagram and Facebook — preserving quality and color from delivery to publish.

Pro tip: For a better voice, tap then use iOS Speak Selection (Settings → Accessibility → Spoken Content → Speak Selection).

Instagram

Turn On High Quality Uploads

  1. 1
    Open Instagram and go to your profile Tap the profile icon in the bottom right corner.
  2. 2
    Open Settings Tap the three lines (≡) in the top right, then tap Settings and privacy.
  3. 3
    Find Data Usage & Media Quality Search for Data usage or navigate to Account → Data usage and media quality.
  4. 4
    Enable "Upload at Highest Quality" Toggle this ON. This tells Instagram to upload the full resolution file instead of compressing it before it even hits their servers.
Why this matters: Instagram compresses uploads by default to save bandwidth. With this toggle off, your professionally produced video or photo gets degraded before anyone even sees it. Turn it on once and leave it on.
Instagram

The Right Way to Post Every Time

  1. 1
    Always post from an iPhone Instagram's iOS app uses a higher quality video encoder than Android or desktop. This is a documented difference — iOS produces a measurably better result for the same file.
  2. 2
    Connect to Wi-Fi before posting Even with high quality uploads enabled, Instagram may throttle file size on cellular. Always post over Wi-Fi to ensure the full file transfers without restrictions.
  3. 3
    Save the original from Google Drive to your Camera Roll first Open Google Drive → find your delivered file → tap the three dots → Download. Wait for it to fully save to your Camera Roll before opening Instagram.
  4. 4
    Post directly from the Camera Roll Never share directly from Google Drive to Instagram. Always go through the Camera Roll — this ensures Instagram reads the native file, not a re-encoded stream.
  5. 5
    Do not apply any filters or edits in Instagram Your content has already been professionally color graded. Any filter — even "Normal" with adjustments — re-processes the image. Post it as delivered.
⚠️ Never screenshot a video or re-export from your phone's editing app and post that. Always start with the original file from Google Drive. Every generation of re-saving loses quality.
Instagram

Reels vs. Feed vs. Stories

  1. 1
    Post video as a Reel, not a Feed video Reels get significantly more algorithmic reach than standard feed videos. If it's a video, it should almost always go up as a Reel.
  2. 2
    Also share your Reel to your Story After posting a Reel, tap Share to Story. This gives your existing followers a second touchpoint and drives them back to the Reel to boost its play count.
  3. 3
    Write a real caption — don't leave it blank Instagram uses captions for indexing and search. A few sentences describing what the video is about — and who it's for — helps it reach the right people.
Facebook · Ads Manager

Turn Off Advantage+ Creative Color Enhancement

Meta automatically applies color and brightness adjustments to your ads through a feature called Advantage+ Creative. For professionally produced content, this needs to be turned off — it will undo your color grade.

  1. 1
    Open Ads Manager and go to your ad Navigate to the Ad level (not Campaign or Ad Set) — this setting only lives at the individual ad level.
  2. 2
    Find the Advantage+ Creative section Scroll down in the ad editor until you see the Advantage+ Creative panel. It may be expanded or collapsed.
  3. 3
    Locate "Adjust Brightness and Contrast" Inside Advantage+ Creative, find the toggle labeled Adjust brightness and contrast. Turn this OFF.
  4. 4
    Disable all other Advantage+ Creative enhancements While you're there, turn off everything else in that section — music, text overlays, image templates. None of these should be applied to finished professional content.
⚠️ Meta turns Advantage+ Creative ON by default for every new ad. You have to manually disable it every time you create a new ad. It does not remember your preference.
Why this matters: Meta applies these adjustments per-viewer — different people see different brightness and color variations of your ad. Your video has already been graded with intention. Letting Meta re-grade it is like handing a finished painting to someone and asking them to touch it up.
Facebook · General

Facebook Posting Best Practices

  1. 1
    Upload video files directly — don't share YouTube or external links Facebook heavily suppresses posts that link offsite. Upload the video file natively to Facebook for full reach.
  2. 2
    Save to Camera Roll first, same as Instagram The same rule applies — download the original from Google Drive, save to Camera Roll, then upload from there. Never share directly from Drive.
  3. 3
    Post on Facebook from a desktop browser when possible Unlike Instagram, Facebook's desktop uploader handles large video files more reliably and gives you more control over thumbnail selection and caption formatting.
  4. 4
    Select a strong thumbnail before publishing Facebook lets you choose or upload a custom thumbnail. Pick a clear, high-quality frame — this is what people see before they press play.
