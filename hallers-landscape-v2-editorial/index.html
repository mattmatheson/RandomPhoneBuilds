For twenty-nine years, the work of Haller's Landscaping & Nursery has held quiet residence on Tennessee's university campuses, municipal parks, and roadside frontages — the kind of large-scale commercial installations that nobody photographs and everybody depends on. The brand is not loud about this. They prefer the work to be.

In 2019 the studio made a deliberate narrowing: stop offering routine maintenance, and concentrate the full weight of the practice on new installation at scale. Landscape, hardscape, irrigation. Done once, done deeply, built to outlast the building it surrounds.

What follows is a brief monograph of that practice — the work, the people, and the principles. It is also, by necessity, a record of the institutions that keep Middle Tennessee in shape.

Chapter 02 Featured Work

Lebanon, Hwy 231 — a full-scope commercial landscape.

Chapter 04 An essay on pervious paving

Pervious paving — the kind nobody asks for and everyone benefits from.

Most commercial hardscape on a campus today is impermeable: stamped concrete, dense aggregate, sealed joints. The water runs off in sheets to a drain, and from the drain into the storm system, and from there into a creek that wasn't designed to absorb a parking lot's worth of runoff every time it rains.

We do it differently. Permeable systems: high-PSI stone, polymeric joint stabilization, ADA-compliant walking surfaces — engineered so that rainwater absorbs back into the soil on-site, where it was supposed to go in the first place.

It is the kind of thing nobody asks for in the RFP. It is also the kind of thing the EPA, your insurance carrier, and the building you're working on will all thank you for in fifteen years.

We've installed it across more than fifty hardscape projects across Middle Tennessee. We will gladly do it on yours.

Chapter 05 The Method

From RFP to ribbon-cutting in four movements.

  1. I. Consultation

    Site walk, soil sampling, drainage assessment, and an honest read on scope. We tell you what we can do, what we can't, and what the budget actually buys.

  2. II. Design

    Custom landscape plan, hardscape layout, irrigation schematic, native species selection. Phasing strategy if the site is live and traffic must continue.

  3. III. Build

    In-house crews, in-house arborists, locally-sourced materials wherever the supply chain permits. A single point of contact for the entire install.

  4. IV. Verify

    Final walk-through, irrigation wet-check, plant warranty through the establishment period. If something fails in the warranty window, we replace it.
