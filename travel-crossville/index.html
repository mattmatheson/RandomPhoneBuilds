Downtown Crossville — Video Locations

  • Palace Theatre (est. 1938)
    Fully restored Art Deco community auditorium. Live music — jazz, blues, country, bluegrass, rock. Great exterior + interior shots.
  • Cumberland County Playhouse
    One of the 10 largest professional theaters in rural America, since 1965. Four stages, 350+ performances/year. 221 Tennessee Ave — heart of downtown.
  • Cumberland Homesteads Tower Museum
    Iconic octagonal stone tower (1937–38). FDR New Deal history. 50,000-gallon water tank, lookout platform at top. On the National Register. Stunning drone subject.
  • Stonehaus Winery
    Tennessee's only winery with statewide distribution. Award-winning wines, tastings, Wine & Paint nights. 2444 Genesis Rd.
  • Local Breweries
    Social Brew (downtown, 10 rotating taps), Swing On Brewing Company (golf-themed), Lilly Pad Hopyard Brewery (scenic views + hiking nearby), Calfkiller Brewing.
  • Downtown Shops & Restaurants
    Main Street area — local boutiques, eateries, coffee shops. Need to scout specific spots with PJ on shoot day.

Outdoors — Video Locations

  • Cumberland Mountain State Park
    Built by the CCC in the 1930s. Byrd Lake, stone bridges, hiking trails through the plateau. Classic Tennessee state park.
  • Obed Wild & Scenic River
    Federally designated Wild & Scenic River. Deep gorge carved through the Cumberland Plateau. Kayaking, rock climbing, swimming holes. Dramatic cliff shots.
  • Lake Tansi
    Private lake community south of Crossville. Boating, fishing, lakefront homes.
  • Byrd Lake / Lake Holiday
    Smaller lakes on Crossville's outskirts. Scenic, quieter, good B-roll locations.
  • Cumberland Plateau Overlooks
    Crossville sits at ~1,900 feet on top of the Cumberland Plateau. Eastern and western escarpments offer dramatic overlook opportunities. Drone paradise.
  • Hiking Trails
    Cumberland Trail, Black Mountain trail system, state park trails. Spring wildflowers in April make for gorgeous footage.

About Crossville

Crossville is the county seat of Cumberland County, Tennessee, sitting on top of the Cumberland Plateau at roughly 1,900 feet elevation. Known as the "Golf Capital of Tennessee" with 10+ courses in the immediate area (Fairfield Glade alone has 5 courses / 90 holes), it's also a gateway to serious outdoor recreation on the plateau.

The Cumberland Plateau is the largest timbered plateau in America. The Obed River carves a dramatic gorge north of town. The climate is moderated by the elevation — cooler summers than Nashville, real seasons, abundant rainfall (55+ inches/year), and enough snow to feel like winter.

The tagline is "Play the Plateau" with the website explorecrossville.com. The logo will include this tagline — PJ confirmed it goes at the end of each commercial with 5–7 seconds of screen time.

Travel Crossville is the tourism arm of the Crossville Chamber of Commerce / Convention & Visitors Bureau. PJ works there managing tourism marketing and content.
