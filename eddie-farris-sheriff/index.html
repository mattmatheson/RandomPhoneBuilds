Eddie Farris

Sheriff of Putnam County, Tennessee

About Record Priorities Allies Endorsements Programs Facebook Contact

Under Sheriff Eddie Farris's leadership, Putnam County has become one of the most professionally run law enforcement agencies in the state of Tennessee — one of only a handful of sheriff's offices to achieve TLEA accreditation, and the first in the region to partner with federal agencies through the ICE 287(g) program.

A native son of Putnam County, Farris has spent more than 35 years in law enforcement, serving at every level from deputy to TBI Special Agent to FBI task force member before returning home to lead the department where his career began.

His administration has returned millions of budgeted funds to the general fund, delivered 10+ consecutive clean audits, and built the county's first training center and firearms range at zero cost to taxpayers — all while expanding community programs, modernizing equipment, and leading the response to some of the worst crises in Putnam County history.

About Eddie Farris

Born & raised in Putnam County

A Cookeville High School standout who earned full scholarships in both baseball and football, Eddie captained the ETSU baseball team and signed a minor league free agency contract before answering his true calling: protecting his community.

With a Criminal Justice degree from East Tennessee State University and training from the FBI, DOJ, TBI, and THP, Eddie Farris brings credentials no other candidate can match.

  • FBI National Academy Graduate — Quantico, Virginia
  • Tennessee Bureau of Investigation Special Agent
  • Tennessee Highway Patrol — Criminal Investigation Division
  • FBI "Safe Streets" Violent Crime Task Force
  • President, Tennessee Sheriffs' Association (2021-22)
  • U.S. Department of Justice Trained
  • Certified Law Enforcement Firearms Instructor & Armorer
  • Member, Putnam County Beer Board
