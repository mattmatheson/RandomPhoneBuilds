About the Sheriff

Born & Raised
in Putnam County

A native son of Putnam County, Eddie Farris has dedicated his entire life to keeping this community safe. From his early days as a deputy in 1989 to his current role as Sheriff, Eddie has built an unmatched career spanning local, state, and federal law enforcement.

A Cookeville High School standout who earned full scholarships in both baseball and football, Eddie went on to captain the ETSU baseball team and even signed a minor league free agency contract. But his true calling was protecting his community — and he's never looked back.

  • ★ FBI National Academy Graduate — Quantico, Virginia
  • ★ Tennessee Bureau of Investigation Special Agent
  • ★ Tennessee Highway Patrol Criminal Investigation Division
  • ★ FBI "Safe Streets" Violent Crime Task Force
  • ★ President, Tennessee Sheriffs' Association (2021-2022)
  • ★ U.S. Department of Justice Trained

Career Timeline

35+ Years of Service

1989
Deputy Sheriff
Began his career under legendary Sheriff Jerry Abston at the Putnam County Sheriff's Department.
1992
First Jail Administrator
Became the first-ever Jail Administrator of the Putnam County Justice Center. Received the highest possible ratings for a direct-supervision jail.
1995
Tennessee Highway Patrol — CID
Selected for the Criminal Investigation Division. Investigated homicides, narcotics, insurance fraud, weapons violations, embezzlement, and internal affairs.
2000
FBI National Academy & Safe Streets Task Force
Graduated from the elite FBI National Academy in Quantico, VA. Selected for the FBI "Safe Streets" Violent Crime Task Force — investigating bank robberies, gang crimes, and corruption.
2004
Assistant Special Agent in Charge
Promoted to lead and supervise agents across the Cookeville and Chattanooga regions for THP Criminal Investigation Division.
2006 — 2014
TBI Special Agent
Focused on dismantling high-level illegal narcotic organizations. Received the HIDTA Case of the Year award in 2011 for a major operation spanning the Upper Cumberland and beyond.
2014 — Present
Sheriff of Putnam County
Sworn in as Sheriff. Led PCSO to its first-ever TLEA accreditation. Served as President of the Tennessee Sheriffs' Association. Implemented SWAT, K-9, Aviation, SRO, and numerous community programs.

His Platform

Standing for What Matters

🛡
Exemplary Law Enforcement
Professional, accredited policing that meets the highest national standards. PCSO is one of the few sheriff's offices in eastern Tennessee to achieve TLEA accreditation — and has maintained it three times.
💪
Tough on Drug Crime
As a former TBI Special Agent who earned the HIDTA Case of the Year award, Eddie brings unmatched narcotics enforcement experience to protect families from the scourge of drugs in the Upper Cumberland.
🏫
Protecting Our Schools
Dedicated School Resource Officers in Putnam County schools. Our children deserve to learn in safety, and the PCSO SRO program ensures trained deputies are present every day.
🇺🇸
Securing Our Borders at Home
PCSO was the first agency in Tennessee to implement the 287(g) Warrant Service Officer program with ICE. Recognized by the ACRU with the Defender Award for enforcing immigration law at the local level.
🤝
Community Trust
From the Citizen's Academy to Coffee with the Sheriff, Farm Watch to Project Lifesaver — Eddie believes law enforcement works best when it works alongside the people it serves.
🙏
Faith & Family Values
Rooted in the values of faith, family, and community that have made Putnam County a great place to live and raise children. Integrity, courage, compassion, professionalism, accountability, and respect.

Fiscal Conservative.
Crisis Leader.
