About the Sheriff

Born & Raised in Putnam County

A native son of Putnam County, Eddie Farris has dedicated his entire life to keeping this community safe. From his days as a deputy in 1989 to his current role as Sheriff, Eddie has built an unmatched career spanning local, state, and federal law enforcement.

A Cookeville High School standout who earned full scholarships in baseball and football, Eddie captained the ETSU baseball team and even signed a minor league free agency contract. But his true calling was protecting his community.

  • FBI National Academy Graduate — Quantico, Virginia
  • Tennessee Bureau of Investigation Special Agent
  • Tennessee Highway Patrol — Criminal Investigation
  • FBI "Safe Streets" Violent Crime Task Force
  • President, Tennessee Sheriffs' Association (2021-22)
  • U.S. Department of Justice Trained
"Earning the Public's Trust, Every Day!"

Career Timeline

35+ Years of Service

1989
Deputy Sheriff
Began his career under Sheriff Jerry Abston at the Putnam County Sheriff's Department.
1992
First Jail Administrator
Became the first-ever Jail Administrator of the Putnam County Justice Center. Highest possible ratings for direct-supervision.
1995
THP Criminal Investigation
Selected for Criminal Investigation Division. Investigated homicides, narcotics, insurance fraud, weapons violations, and internal affairs.
2000
FBI National Academy & Task Force
Graduated from the elite FBI National Academy, Quantico. Selected for FBI "Safe Streets" Violent Crime Task Force — bank robberies, gang crimes, corruption.
2004
Asst. Special Agent in Charge
Promoted to lead agents across Cookeville and Chattanooga regions for THP Criminal Investigation Division.
2006 — 2014
TBI Special Agent
Focused on dismantling high-level narcotic organizations. HIDTA Case of the Year 2011. Murder-for-hire, public corruption, undercover with FBI, DEA, ATF.
2014 — Present
Sheriff of Putnam County
First-ever TLEA accreditation. President of Tennessee Sheriffs' Association. Built SWAT, K-9, Aviation, SRO, and community programs. Four deputies nominated to FBI National Academy.

His Platform

Standing for What Matters

Exemplary Law Enforcement
Professional, accredited policing at the highest national standards. PCSO is one of the few sheriff's offices in eastern Tennessee to achieve TLEA accreditation — maintained three times.
Tough on Drug Crime
Former TBI Special Agent with HIDTA Case of the Year. Unmatched narcotics enforcement protecting families from drugs in the Upper Cumberland.
Protecting Our Schools
Dedicated School Resource Officers in every Putnam County school. Our children deserve to learn in safety with trained deputies present every day.
Securing Borders at Home
First agency in Tennessee to implement ICE 287(g) Warrant Service Officer program. ACRU Defender Award for enforcing immigration law locally.
Community Trust
Citizen's Academy, Coffee with the Sheriff, Farm Watch, Project Lifesaver — law enforcement works best when it works alongside the people it serves.
Faith & Family Values
Rooted in faith, family, and community. Integrity, courage, compassion, professionalism, accountability, and respect — the core values of PCSO.

Proven Results

Fiscal Conservative. Crisis Leader.

Taxpayer Money Returned
Returned millions of budgeted funds to the general fund. 10+ consecutive clean audits from the Tennessee Comptroller's Office.
Built at Zero Cost
Built Putnam County's first Training Center and Firearms Range at no cost to taxpayers. Millions in federal grants and surplus equipment via 1033 program.
Crisis Tested
Led response to the 2020 Super Tuesday Tornado (19 lives lost), 2015 Ice Storm, COVID-19 pandemic, and a Presidential Visit. Federal Disaster Declaration reimbursement secured.
Jail Excellence
TCI Tier II accreditation and first-ever PREA compliance. Work release, GED, parenting classes, Celebrate Recovery, career certificate programs.
Technology Upgrades
Body cameras, upgraded vehicles, body armor, dispatch, IT, patrol systems. Region's first and only Reality Based Scenario Training Center.
Federal Task Forces
Active with FBI Violent Crime, Joint Terrorism, ATF, DEA/HIDTA, U.S. Marshals Fugitive, and ICE 287(g). Federal resources protecting local families.
