Quick Reference — On-Set Research

Center Hill Lake — Stats

18,220 acres 64 miles long 415 mi shoreline 190 ft deepest Built 1948 31 boat ramps 9 marinas "Tennessee's Emerald Gem"

Harbor Pointe

  • Gated ridgetop community, Silver Point TN (DeKalb County)
  • ~1 hour east of Nashville via I-40 East to exit 268
  • Lots back up to Corps of Engineers property / private cove
  • Underground utilities, paved roads, fiber optic
  • Connects directly into Edgar Evins State Park via trailhead
  • harborpointecenterhill.com

Edgar Evins State Park

  • 6,000 acres along Center Hill Lake — adjacent to Harbor Pointe
  • 300-slip full-service marina + Earl's Fish Camp restaurant
  • 12 miles of trails across 7 routes
  • Split-level cabins overlooking water, pool access
  • Pontoon boat rides, kayak/canoe rentals

Caney Fork River

  • Cold dam-released water = one of the best trout fisheries in the SE
  • Rainbow, brown, and brook trout — TWRA stocks 100,000+ fish/year
  • Top fly fishing destination in the Southeast
  • Kayakers travel from across the region for the waterfalls and hydraulics
  • Below the dam: deep gorge + "Blue Hole" (walleye spawning site)

Upper Cumberland Waterfalls

  • 150+ waterfalls within 40 miles of Cookeville
  • Fall Creek Falls — 256 ft, one of tallest in eastern US (26,000-acre park)
  • Burgess Falls — 130+ ft plunge, Sparta TN
  • Cummins Falls — 75 ft, 10 min north of Cookeville
  • Rock Island — Twin Falls, 80-ft drop (883 acres)
  • Greeter Falls (Savage Gulf) — 15-ft upper + 50-ft lower into plunge pool

Nearby Towns

  • Smithville — ~4,300 pop. County seat. Home of the Fiddlers' Jamboree (25,000+ spectators). Appalachian Center for Craft (TN Tech) overlooks Center Hill Lake.
  • Cookeville — ~35,000 pop. Tennessee Tech University. 100+ restaurants. Cummins Falls 10 min from downtown. 4th fastest-growing micropolitan area in the US.
  • Nashville BNA — ~58–60 miles, about 1 hour 9 min drive
