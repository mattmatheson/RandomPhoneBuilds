First things first

What Is This Plan?

You have years of teaching experience and a head full of great ideas — activities, worksheets, lesson plans, games, and strategies that actually work in a classroom.

Teachers Pay Teachers (TpT) is a website where teachers sell those exact kinds of resources to other teachers. Over 7 million educators use it. Some sellers make a full-time income from it. Many make a nice side income of a few hundred to a few thousand dollars a month.

The problem? Turning your ideas into polished, professional products takes time and tech skills — designing layouts, formatting PDFs, writing descriptions, making thumbnails.

That's where this plan comes in. You share your ideas in the simplest way possible — a voice memo, a text message, or scribbles on paper. Matt and his AI assistant (Claude) handle everything else: designing the product, writing the listing, and getting it uploaded to your TpT store.

What you actually have to do

Your Part: 15 Minutes Every Two Weeks

This is designed so your part is as easy as possible. No computer skills needed. No software to learn. No accounts to manage.

Pick whichever method feels natural:

Option A: Voice Memo

Open the voice memo app on your phone. Talk through your idea like you're telling a friend about it. Send it to Matt. Done.

Option B: Text Message

Text Matt your idea. It doesn't have to be organized or pretty. Just get the idea out of your head and into a text.

Option C: Paper

Write it down or sketch it out on paper. Matt snaps a photo. That's your contribution done.

What to include (in your own words):

  • The idea — What is it? A worksheet? A game? A set of task cards? A full lesson?
  • Who's it for? — What grade level? What subject?
  • What problem does it solve? — Why would another teacher want this? (Example: "Kids always zone out during fractions — this makes it a game so they actually pay attention.")
  • How would you use it? — Walk us through how you'd use it in your classroom. This is the gold. This is your expertise talking.

That's it. You don't upload anything. You don't design anything. You don't write product descriptions. You talk or text, and we handle the rest.

What you get back

What Gets Created From Your Idea

Every idea you send us comes back as a complete, ready-to-sell package:

  • The product itself — A professionally designed PDF (or PowerPoint/Google Slides file) with clean formatting, clear instructions, and answer keys where needed
  • A store thumbnail — The eye-catching cover image that shoppers see when browsing TpT
  • Preview pages — 4-6 sample pages so buyers can see what they're getting before they purchase
  • A written listing — Title, description, and keywords optimized so teachers can find your product when they search
  • A "thank you" page — Included in every product, asking buyers to leave a review (reviews are everything on TpT)

Matt reviews everything before it goes live. You can review too if you want — but you don't have to.

Common questions

Things You Might Be Wondering

"Do I need to learn any software?"

No. You don't need to open PowerPoint, Canva, or any design tool. Your only job is to share your ideas. We do everything else.

"What if my idea isn't fully formed yet?"

That's fine. Half-baked ideas are welcome. You can say "I have this thing I do with flashcards where..." and we'll shape it into a complete product. The rougher the idea, the more we can help.

"Is the AI going to make it sound like a robot wrote it?"

No. Claude is a tool, like a very fast graphic designer or assistant. The ideas, the teaching strategies, and the classroom experience are all yours. Claude just makes them look professional. Your voice and expertise are the product — Claude is the packaging.

"Can I review everything before it goes live?"

Absolutely. Nothing goes up without your approval. Matt will send you previews of every product. If something doesn't feel right or doesn't match how you'd actually use it in a classroom, we fix it.

"How long until I start making money?"

Most TpT sellers see their first sales within the first month. Consistent income ($200+/month) typically kicks in around 20-30 products. At our pace of 2 per month, that's roughly 10-15 months — but we might move faster than that.

"What does it cost to start?"

$59.95 per year for a TpT Premium seller account. That's it. Claude is already set up on Matt's computer. No other subscriptions or tools needed.

"What if a product doesn't sell?"

No risk. There's no cost per product and no inventory. If something doesn't sell, it just sits there. But even slow sellers add up over time — and we can always update or rebundle them.
