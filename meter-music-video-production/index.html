Creative Direction

Overarching Vibe: Early 2000s dark alternative — Evanescence era. Y2K alt-rock shot authentically in that spirit, not ironic or retro-filtered. The video has TWO contrasting visual worlds that intercut, mirroring how Linkin Park's "Papercut" video alternates between its warm living room and cold basement.

Day 1 — The Subway, Nashville (April 11, Night Shoot)

Primary Reference: Baxter — "Television" (Warner Records, late 90s)

This video is 85% of the target aesthetic for Day 1. Matt sent it directly to Paul (gaffer) with: "going for this kind of vibe" and "focus on the dark blue y2k stuff from that Baxter video."

  • Color: Deep teal/cyan-blue — everything drenched in cold blue-green. The teal comes from fluorescent tubes mounted on walls. No warm tones.
  • Lighting: 100% practical/motivated. Paul's 8 tube set mounted along subway car walls, set to cyan/teal. Flickering is key — Matt specifically wants that unsettling flicker. Fog/haze critical to catch tube light and create volume.
  • Camera: Long tracking shots down the length of the subway car. Close-up portraits with blue tube bokeh behind. Low-angle foot tracking through corridors. Handheld with intentional steadiness — organic, not chaotic.
  • Mood: Dark, institutional, fever dream. Keep it DARK — don't fill shadow, let faces go to silhouette. The darkness is the aesthetic.
  • Set pieces: CRT televisions on rolling carts if available (mirrors "Television" motif). White wardrobe picks up the teal and glows.
  • Schedule: 4pm arrival, shooting sunset to ~midnight. No power on site — all generator powered.

Day 2 — Cortney & Luke's House (April 12)

Primary Reference: Linkin Park — "Papercut" (Official HD, 2000)

The living room performance scenes are the template. Matt told Paul: "Linkin park video is mostly for the second location the next day."

  • Color: Warm amber/golden — table lamps create a warm base. Slight desaturation but overall warm-neutral. Vintage quality with milky shadows.
  • Lighting: Multiple table lamps scattered around the living room as primary light source. Evening/night, lamps only, no studio lighting. The lamps light faces from low/side angles — unusual shadow patterns. Keep it warm but not bright.
  • Camera: Handheld push-ins on faces during energy peaks. Whip pans between band members — embrace the motion blur. Extreme close-ups (profiles, jawlines, mouths, eyes). Wide establishing shots of room with all members.
  • Pacing: Start with intimate chill moments — acoustic guitar on couch, band hanging out. Build to full performance energy. Mirrors how Papercut escalates from calm verse to chaotic chorus.
  • Production Design: Whatever is on Cortney & Luke's walls becomes part of the visual language. Dress the set if needed — lamps, furniture, art all matter.

Transition & Cutaway Language (Both Days)

  • Whip pan motion blur — shoot intentional whip pans as transitions (the blur IS the edit)
  • Flash cuts between worlds — cold subway footage intercut into warm house scenes and vice versa
  • ECU of faces — profiles, jawlines, eyes, mouths. Fill the frame.
  • ECU of hands — on instruments, on surfaces, on objects
  • Detail/texture shots — tile, metal, fabric, glass, faucets, pipes
  • Face against glass — ghostly, eerie, frosted effect (from Papercut)
  • Intentional motion blur abstracts — shoot some pure-blur frames for edit transitions
  • The Day 1 → Day 2 contrast (cold teal → warm amber) is the visual thesis of the whole video
