01 — The Concept

Low-budget Fuse TV, 2003.
But for Meter, right now.

A live-show document of Meter at The Cobra, cut like a segment from peak-era Fuse TV. Host Benny Method wears glasses, plays a goofy charmer, and interviews fans in the crowd and the band backstage. The A-cam is a camcorder (auto-exposure hunting, soft focus — that's the look). The B-cam grades down to B&W and drops frames for cheesy cutaways. Rock stingers punch every transition. A meter.tv bug pulses in the lower-right corner — not all the time, just when the segment needs it. The doc you're reading demonstrates a bunch of the visual tropes inline — scroll down to section 05 to see them play.

02 — Core Reference

05 — The Visual Playbook

Ten Visual Tropes, Demonstrated Live

These are the ingredients. Hit 6-8 of these across the cut and it'll feel unmistakably 2003. The left-side panels are live CSS mockups — these are playing right now in this document so you can see each effect in motion.

METER.TV
01 Essential

The meter.tv Network Bug

Bottom-right corner. Red box with white text. Animates IN at the top of each new segment with a scale-up and slight glow, sits for 20-30 seconds, then slides OUT. Never stays on the whole time — that's modern. Real 2003 bugs came and went.

Benny's third time crowd-surfing this MONTH
02 Signature

Pop-Up Commentary Bubbles

White fill, black outline, red drop-shadow, bold factoid inside, arrow pointing to the subject. Animates in with a squash-and-stretch "bloop" + matching SFX. 3-5 seconds on screen max. Limit 1-2 per segment — don't over-clutter.

BENNY vs.THE CROWD
03 Essential

Title Card Slams

Bold italic block type with chunky drop-shadows, rotated a few degrees off-axis, slams in on-beat with a stinger. Text like "BENNY vs. THE CROWD", "MEETING METER", "MOSH PIT REPORT". Hold 1-2 seconds then cut to the scene.

JIMMY — 21 Front Row Moshling
04 Per-Interview

Tilted Lower-Third Name Plates

Rotated 2-5° off-axis. Bold sans-serif. Red accent bar on the left. Name in white, role in a black sub-pill. Slides in from the left. Every fan interview should get one. Give them made-up job titles: "Front Row Moshling", "Professional Singalong", "Cousin's Band".

05 Essential

Rock Stinger Transitions

0.5-1.5 seconds total. Power chord or cymbal crash, white frame flash, logo slam, cut to next segment. You already have two stinger MP3s in the project folder — see section 06 to play them.

06 Cutaway Only

B-Cam B&W + Low Frame Rate

Desaturate to B&W (or heavy cyan tint). Drop to 12-15fps via frame-blending. Add a vignette + subtle grain. Use as 0.5-1.5s inserts between A-cam beats — never as sustained shots. Dutch angles recommended.

07 Use Sparingly

VHS / Camcorder Overlays

Tracking bars, horizontal noise lines, timecode burn-in, "REC ●" top-right, battery icon, date stamp. A little goes a long way — two VHS glitches in a 3-minute piece is plenty. More than that and it feels like a TikTok filter.

08 The Classic Move

Freeze-Frame + Handwritten Arrow

Zoom-punch into a fan's face, hold 0.5s, freeze frame, circle graphic + arrow + handwritten label ("THIS GUY RULES", "DRUNK", "OWES MONEY"). Cut out hard. This is the Tom Green / Jackass move. Classic for a reason.

09 Voiceover

The Gravel Announcer

Pitched-down male narrator, heavy compression, radio-EQ. Used only for segment intros / upcoming-next teases. Pitch your own voice down in post and add a slapback delay — works fine. Keep lines short and punchy.

10 Punchline Tool

Freeze + Record-Scratch

On a weird moment: freeze the frame, record-scratch SFX, Benny VO commentary ("Yeah… that happened."). Classic VH1 "I Love the 90s" move. Breaks the 4th wall. Don't overdo it — once or twice per cut is the sweet spot.

10 — Dos & Don'ts

Ground Rules

What makes the era — and what kills it.

DO

  • Let the A-cam camcorder footage look rough — auto-exposure hunting, soft focus, occasional overexposure. That's the look.
  • Use hard cuts 90% of the time; save stingers for major beats.
  • Let Benny be loud, earnest, and a little cringy. That's the point.
  • Mix lo-fi (camcorder, VHS) with hi-fi (clean B-cam, crisp stingers) — 2003 was the transition era and the contrast IS the aesthetic.
  • Include at least one freeze-frame-with-arrow joke per segment.

DON'T

  • Use modern smooth motion graphics — it screams 2020s.
  • Use long dissolves or cinematic transitions — kills the energy.
  • Overuse VHS glitches — 2-3 in a 3-min piece is the ceiling.
  • Let any shot breathe longer than 2-3 seconds unless it's a band performance beat.
  • Use clean modern fonts. Go bold italic, heavy drop-shadow, marker, or nothing.

Media Team Quick Reference

  • The look is DIY — not polish. If it feels like you're trying too hard, you are. The magic is that it looks thrown-together.
  • Velocity over composition. 2003 TV had edits every 1-2 seconds. Lean into that.
  • Graphics are loud. Bold italic, drop-shadows, red/yellow, rotation. No subtlety.
  • Benny is a character. Not a host. Lean into the glasses + goofball bit — never break.
  • Two stingers already in-folder at meter-cobra-nashville/.
  • Host model: Steven Smith (SURS) + Tom Green. Earnest chaos.
