I. Statement of Facts

On or about March 14, 2026, the Complainant entered Game X Change located at 620 S. Jefferson Avenue, Cookeville, Tennessee 38501, and purchased approximately Two Hundred Dollars ($200.00) worth of Blu-ray motion pictures. Among these purchases was one (1) Hunger Games Double Feature Blu-ray, marketed and sold as containing both The Hunger Games (2012) and The Hunger Games: Catching Fire (2013).

Upon returning to his residence and opening the aforementioned Blu-ray case, the Complainant discovered that the case contained only one (1) disc — specifically, the second film. The first disc was not present. The product labeled as a "double feature" was, in material fact, a single feature. The Complainant received fifty percent (50%) of the product for one hundred percent (100%) of the purchase price.

The Complainant notes for the record that this is not the first time he has received an incomplete product from this location. On prior occasions, the Complainant chose not to pursue the matter, absorbing the financial loss in the interest of maintaining a positive commercial relationship with the store. This forbearance should not be interpreted as acquiescence to a pattern of defective merchandise.

II. The Return Visit

On March 16, 2026, the Complainant returned to Game X Change to report the missing disc and request its replacement. What followed was a customer service interaction that can only be described as hostile, inefficient, and logically incoherent. The events are recounted as follows:

  1. The Complainant approached the counter and calmly explained the situation. Staff responded by suggesting that the Complainant should have inspected the product in his vehicle before departing the premises — effectively placing the burden of quality assurance on the consumer rather than the retailer.
  2. Staff requested a receipt. The Complainant does not retain thermal paper receipts due to well-documented health concerns regarding Bisphenol A (BPA) exposure, a position supported by the European Chemicals Agency (ECHA) and multiple peer-reviewed studies. The Complainant instead offered a timestamped photograph sent via iMessage to a third party at the time of purchase, clearly depicting all items being purchased at the Game X Change counter.
  3. Staff declined to accept the photographic evidence and instead attempted to locate the transaction in their point-of-sale system. Staff advised this process would be time-consuming. The Complainant asked whether the system supported a search function or whether artificial intelligence could expedite the process. This question — asked in good faith — prompted a staff member to become visibly agitated, declare that he would "never use AI," and maintain a hostile demeanor for the remainder of the interaction.
  4. After approximately ten (10) minutes, staff located the transaction and proceeded to read each item aloud, asking the Complainant to confirm each purchase individually. Every item confirmed matched the photograph the Complainant had presented at the outset.
  5. Rather than providing the missing disc — which, by logical necessity, must still be in the store's possession — staff issued store credit. The Complainant explicitly requested the disc itself, noting that the store would otherwise be left with an unsellable loose disc and the customer with an incomplete case. Staff refused, stating: "No sir, we're not going to do that."
