The short version

You've got two ads running right now — one built to grow your Instagram following, and one built to drive Spotify streams for "Sin of Loneliness." Both reach a broad audience across the US, Canada, UK & Ireland — not locked to one genre — and both use your video (with a vertical cut made specifically for Stories & Reels).

1What was going wrong

The old ad looked like it was "only reaching your current followers." It turned out the targeting wasn't the real problem — two other things were:

  • The account had run out of money. The ad account runs on a prepaid balance, and it was down to about $10. The old ad quietly spent that and then stopped delivering — so it never had the budget to reach new people beyond your warmest audience.
  • The budget was too thin for the goal. A few dollars a day spread across three continents can't find new fans. It collapses back to the people Instagram already knows follow you.

2What we changed

  • Added funds so the ads can actually run.
  • Rebuilt the ads around your video — not a photo — with proper goals and a real audience.
  • Split it into two ads so one chases followers and one chases streams, instead of asking a single ad to do both.
  • Added your vertical clip so Instagram Stories & Reels show a full-screen, properly-formatted vertical video, while the feed shows the landscape version.
  • Opened up the audience so it isn't locked to one genre — your music crosses rock, country, metal and beyond, so we let Meta find that whole mix.

5What to expect

  • Give it 24–48 hours. New ads go through a "learning" period while the system figures out who responds — early numbers aren't the real picture.
  • Followers won't spike overnight. The follower ad drives people to your profile; some follow, some just look. Steady beats sudden.
  • Streams build gradually as the "Listen now" taps land on your Spotify page.
  • The ads pause on their own when the funds run out — nothing runs away with your money.
